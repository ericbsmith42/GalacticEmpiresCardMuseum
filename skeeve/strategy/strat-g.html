Gekonauak

with suggestions from David S. Auer

Geko decks have a problem. A problem similar to that of Space Dragon, Orgon and psy decks: their ships are easier to kill than those of any empire given equal strength cards. You need to use the various cards which modify weapon volleys against Geko ships: the Geko Gnats, T7 Planet - Gekonauak Outpost and S8 Geko King all reduce incoming volleys. Consider using the various strengths of Space Remoras on your Gnats to bring them back to hand when they would otherwise be discarded. Lastly, fill out your fleet with some generic or minor empire ships to give you a few ships with a little more staying power than the typical Geko ship.

Key cards:

  • C3 Time-travelling Arms Dealer (promotional card)
  • C10 Privateer fully engage its location as a card action
  • C10 Time-travelling Arms Dealer (promotional card)
  • L8 Call It Good engages the whole bloody fleet
  • M1 Geko Gnats
  • M2 Geko Gnats
  • R/O6 Surprise Maneuver allows a disengaged ship to fire its heavy weapons in response to incoming weapons fire
  • O7 Surprise Attack
  • T3 Dimensional Moon play a ship which immediately goes out of play until next turn, when you can engage it!
  • T7 Planet - Gekonauak Outpost

Consider these cards:

  • E8 Time Shield hide a stack of cards on the turn played until the next turn, when you can use it
  • S7 Andromeda Bound Munitions Cruiser supplies ammunition
  • T5 Star supplies 5 energy for engagement
  • T10 Intergalactic Void allows one ship per turn to be played in the engaged position
  • T10 Super-massive Star supplies 10 energy for engagement
Also consider minor empire and generic ships which can be used to defend your Sector HQ and provide supply to your Gekos:
  • S3 Independant Tug
  • S4 Converted Freighter
  • S5 Ficticious Ship
  • S6 Armed Tug

Cards for Geko Maneuvering Systems:

  • R/L6 Lucky Maneuver
  • O3 Tight Maneuver
  • R/O4 Formation Maneuver
  • R/O5 Evasive Maneuver
  • R/O5 Fleet Maneuver
  • R/O6 Crash Maneuver
I have found that many of the manuever cards, especially R/O6 Crash Maneuver are not really as effective as I would like.
